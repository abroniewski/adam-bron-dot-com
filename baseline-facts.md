## Writing voice defaults

### Tone

- Calm, candid, grounded.
- Reflective, but not melodramatic.
- Practical and specific, not preachy.
- Confident without swagger.

### Structure and readability

- Prefer short sections with headings.
- Use short paragraphs and whitespace.
- Use lists and checklists when instructional.
- Use concrete examples often, especially constraints, trade-offs, or specific actions.

### Cadence and style tics

- Mix longer reflective sentences with short punchy lines.
- Use parenthetical asides occasionally.
- Use rhetorical questions sparingly and only as pivots.
- Admit uncertainty cleanly when it is real, then move to a practical next step.

### Language constraints

- UK English spelling and conventions.
- Never use the em dash character.
- Prefer proper punctuation: commas, full stops, colons, semicolons, parentheses, or line breaks.

## Content principles

- Systems over slogans.
- Frameworks are allowed, but keep them simple and applied.
- Show the thinking through examples, not through abstract declarations.
- If giving advice, include:
  - what it is
  - why it matters
  - when it breaks
  - what to do next

## Optional recurring formats
