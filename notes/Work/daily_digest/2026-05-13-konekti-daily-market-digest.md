# Konekti Daily Market Digest — 2026-05-13

## 1) What stood out
The clearest signal today is that enterprise vendors are no longer separating “AI capability” from “governed execution”: [UiPath is productizing coding agents inside a governed orchestration layer](https://ir.uipath.com/news/detail/447/uipath-becomes-first-business-orchestration-automation-platform-with-native-integration-for-coding-agents-unlocking-enterprise-transformation-at-scale), while [SAP is launching an Autonomous Enterprise stack that ties agents to process context, data, and governance](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-sapphire-sap-unveils-autonomous-enterprise/).

## 2) Coherent storyline
The market is converging on one architecture: semantically rich business data + secure runtime controls + workflow orchestration. [SAP’s AI Platform now centers a knowledge graph and governed agent environment](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-sapphire-sap-unveils-autonomous-enterprise/), [SAP and NVIDIA are co-defining secure agent execution with policy and audit hooks](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/secure-ai-agents-how-sap-and-nvidia-co-define-enterprise-grade-agent-execution/), and [SAP + AWS are pushing zero-copy access to semantically rich SAP data for AI agents](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-aws-next-generation-ai-bi-directional-zero-copy-data-sharing-sap-bdc/). The implication is simple: buyers increasingly expect agentic workflows to be production-safe, data-grounded, and measurable from day one.

## 3) Most impacted stakeholder
**COO / transformation lead.** They are the ones most exposed if agentic workflows look promising in demos but fail in live handoffs, controls, or auditability; that is exactly why [UiPath is emphasizing reliability and enterprise guardrails after the code is generated](https://www.uipath.com/blog/product-and-updates/introducing-uipath-for-coding-agents), and why [Amazon Finance is showcasing a tightly governed AI workflow for regulatory inquiries with observability and dedicated knowledge bases](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/machine-learning/how-amazon-finance-streamlines-regulatory-inquiries-by-using-generative-ai-on-aws/).

## 4) Competitive watch (Celonis / Signavio / UiPath / Apromore)
Today’s strongest direct competitive signals came from UiPath and SAP/Signavio-adjacent SAP. [UiPath’s new coding-agent announcement](https://ir.uipath.com/news/detail/447/uipath-becomes-first-business-orchestration-automation-platform-with-native-integration-for-coding-agents-unlocking-enterprise-transformation-at-scale) matters because it reframes the battleground from automation design to governed automation lifecycle management. The accompanying [UiPath blog post](https://www.uipath.com/blog/product-and-updates/introducing-uipath-for-coding-agents) doubles down on retry logic, vaulting, RBAC, and state persistence as the real differentiators. On the SAP side, [the Autonomous Enterprise launch](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-sapphire-sap-unveils-autonomous-enterprise/) and [the SAP–NVIDIA secure runtime collaboration](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/secure-ai-agents-how-sap-and-nvidia-co-define-enterprise-grade-agent-execution/) show how Signavio-adjacent process narratives are being pulled into a larger agent-execution and governance story. No strong same-day primary Celonis or Apromore source surfaced in the accessible fresh set, which itself is a signal: today’s narrative momentum was with vendors making bigger “execution + governance” platform claims.

## 5) Agentic data-model tooling watch (Illumex + peers)
The strongest adjacent signal was not Illumex specifically, but the market moving in Illumex’s direction. [SAP + AWS zero-copy data sharing through SAP Business Data Cloud](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-aws-next-generation-ai-bi-directional-zero-copy-data-sharing-sap-bdc/) reinforces that semantically rich, governed business data is becoming the substrate for enterprise agents. [SAP + Palantir’s AI-supported migration tooling](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-palantir-enhance-partnership-ai-supported-data-migration-tooling/) adds another layer: enterprises want AI to accelerate modernization, but only when the data model, process context, and validation path remain intact. In adjacent enterprise workflow stacks, [ServiceNow now pitches Process Mining as a process-intelligence layer for AI Control Tower, with agent execution logs and governance visibility](https://www.servicenow.com/products/process-mining.html), which further confirms that control context is moving closer to the runtime.

## 6) Key implications
- Konekti should keep positioning around **governed execution**, not generic AI automation, because [SAP](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-sapphire-sap-unveils-autonomous-enterprise/) and [UiPath](https://ir.uipath.com/news/detail/447/uipath-becomes-first-business-orchestration-automation-platform-with-native-integration-for-coding-agents-unlocking-enterprise-transformation-at-scale) are both training the market to ask about control, auditability, and production readiness.
- Data-model access is becoming a frontline GTM issue, as shown by [SAP + AWS zero-copy business data access](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-aws-next-generation-ai-bi-directional-zero-copy-data-sharing-sap-bdc/) and [SAP + Palantir migration acceleration](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-palantir-enhance-partnership-ai-supported-data-migration-tooling/).
- Procurement/compliance buyers are becoming more relevant, with [AWS elevating AI sovereignty](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/security/enabling-ai-sovereignty-on-aws/) and [Coupa buying Rossum to push autonomous spend management](https://www.coupa.com/newsroom/coupa-acquires-rossum-to-accelerate-end-to-end-autonomous-spend-management/).
