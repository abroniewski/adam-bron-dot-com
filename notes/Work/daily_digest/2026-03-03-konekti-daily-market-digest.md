# Konekti Daily Market Digest — 2026-03-03

## 1) What stood out
The clearest shift today is that enterprise AI adoption conversations are being led by **risk and controls first**, not just speed, with both [ZDNet’s insider-threat framing](https://www.zdnet.com/article/enterprise-ai-agents-insider-threat/) and [Help Net Security’s governance warning](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/03/03/enterprise-ai-agent-security-2026/) pointing to tighter security gates before agentic rollout.

## 2) Coherent storyline
A practical storyline is emerging: transformation leaders are pushing AI-led operating-model change (see [Deloitte + ServiceNow’s report release](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/transformation-as-a-living-discipline-deloitte-and-servicenow-report-reveals-key-trends-connecting-the-ai-fueled-enterprise-302701269.html)), while security narratives around browser-embedded and autonomous agents are simultaneously getting sharper (see [Gemini-panel extension escalation coverage](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/new-chrome-vulnerability-let-malicious.html) and [quantum-resilient certificate work in Chrome](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/google-develops-merkle-tree.html)); together, this means buyers will favor vendors that combine automation outcomes with credible governance posture.

## 3) Most impacted stakeholder (single)
The most impacted stakeholder today is the **COE Lead for process/data transformation**, because they must defend AI-program scale decisions to security and procurement while still delivering measurable throughput gains, and this tension is visible in enterprise proof points like [Mondelēz’s Celonis-backed SAP modernization path](https://www.theregister.com/2026/02/27/mondelez_sap_migration_celonis/).
