# Konekti Daily Market Digest — 1 May 2026

## 1) What stood out (short)
Celonis is still pushing the strongest “process intelligence as AI control layer” story today, especially through its [roadmap framing](https://www.celonis.com/blog/celonis-product-roadmap-charting-the-course-to-the-superfluid-enterprise) and [process-intelligence narrative](https://www.celonis.com/blog/manual-chaos-to-autonomous-elegance-business-process-evolution-and-why-ai-needs-a-brain-called-pi), while UiPath and Apromore are both keeping agentic/automation messaging warm via their [blog hub](https://www.uipath.com/blog) and [AI copilot updates](https://apromore.com/newsroom/apromore-expands-ai-portfolio-with-performance-insights-copilot).

## 2) Coherent storyline (brief)
The market is converging on a simple message: AI only gets bought when it is anchored to process context, governance, and measurable outcomes — which is why Celonis is leaning on customer proof like [Carrefour’s procurement use case](https://www.celonis.com/blog/retailer-carrefour-is-transforming-procurement-with-a-process-approach-to-generative-ai), while UiPath keeps the ecosystem visible through its [DEVCON announcement](https://www.uipath.com/newsroom/uipath-announces-devcon-rpa-developer-community-event) and Apromore keeps climbing the feature/credibility ladder with [analyst validation](https://apromore.com/newsroom/apromore-named-leader-and-star-performer-in-everest-groups-2025-process-mining-products-peak-matrix).

## 3) Most impacted stakeholder (single)
**Enterprise operations and transformation leaders** — they need a vendor stack that can prove AI value, not just demo it.

## 4) Competitive watch (Celonis / SAP Signavio / UiPath / Apromore)
- **Celonis** is reinforcing the moat with a [product roadmap post](https://www.celonis.com/blog/celonis-product-roadmap-charting-the-course-to-the-superfluid-enterprise), a [PI positioning article](https://www.celonis.com/blog/manual-chaos-to-autonomous-elegance-business-process-evolution-and-why-ai-needs-a-brain-called-pi), and customer storytelling around [procurement transformation](https://www.celonis.com/blog/retailer-carrefour-is-transforming-procurement-with-a-process-approach-to-generative-ai).
- **UiPath** is keeping the market warm through its [blog hub](https://www.uipath.com/blog) and [DEVCON community event](https://www.uipath.com/newsroom/uipath-announces-devcon-rpa-developer-community-event), which suggests continued emphasis on developer pull and platform stickiness.
- **Apromore** is moving the AI narrative forward with [Performance Insights Copilot](https://apromore.com/newsroom/apromore-expands-ai-portfolio-with-performance-insights-copilot) and external credibility via [Everest Group recognition](https://apromore.com/newsroom/apromore-named-leader-and-star-performer-in-everest-groups-2025-process-mining-products-peak-matrix).
- **SAP Signavio** is not giving us a new primary signal in this scan, so I’d treat it as steady-state unless the next sweep turns up a dated newsroom item.

## 5) Agentic data-model tooling watch (Illumex + peers)
Agentic tooling is still tilting toward governed semantic layers: Google’s [agent-skills post](https://developers.googleblog.com/closing-the-knowledge-gap-with-agent-skills/) shows how lightweight knowledge overlays are being productized, which keeps pressure on data-model vendors to explain how they stay authoritative in an agent-first stack.

## 6) Key implications (max 3 bullets)
- **Process context is now a buying requirement:** the Celonis set of posts shows the market reward for making AI feel operational, not abstract.
- **Community and ecosystem motions still matter:** UiPath’s event-led cadence suggests platform vendors are using developer gravity as a proxy for traction.
- **Credibility signals are compressing into one page:** Apromore’s AI features plus analyst validation show buyers want proof of momentum and proof of fit.
