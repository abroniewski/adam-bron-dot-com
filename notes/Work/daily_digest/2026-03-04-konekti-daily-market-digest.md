# Konekti Daily Market Digest — 2026-03-04

## 1) What stood out
The biggest same-day signal is that process platforms are packaging AI guidance into execution workflows: SAP made [Joule generally available inside SAP Signavio](https://news.sap.com/japan/2026/03/0303_process-conversation-joule-sap-signavio-solutions-generally-available/), while UiPath pushed a concrete [AgentOps operating model](https://www.uipath.com/blog/ai/agent-ops-operationalizing-ai-agents-for-enterprise).

## 2) Coherent storyline
The market is shifting from "AI feature shipping" to "AI operations with governance": SAP is framing [continuous transformation orchestration](https://news.sap.com/2026/03/orchestrating-continuous-transformation-stronger-business-outcomes/), UiPath is pushing [adoption playbooks for agentic AI](https://www.uipath.com/blog/ai/cn/adopting-agentic-ai-2026-things-you-can-do-right-now), and data stack vendors are making models executable through workflow interfaces like Fabric's [programmatic Power Query preview](https://blog.fabric.microsoft.com/en-US/blog/execute-power-query-programmatically-in-microsoft-fabric/).

## 3) Most impacted stakeholder (single)
**COE Lead (Process + Data Automation)** is most impacted because they now have to prove value and control at the same time: contract constraints can stall deployment (per [Reuters on AI contract restrictions](https://www.reuters.com/business/ai-contract-restrictions-could-threaten-military-missions-us-official-says-2026-03-03/)), while buyers demand stronger risk posture (see [CPO readiness findings](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/industry-study-cpos-are-taking-charge-of-ai-risk-and-growth-in-2026-302703015.html)).
