# Konekti Daily Market Digest — 2026-05-04

## 1) What stood out
The market is still converging on the same message: [process intelligence](https://www.celonis.com/insights/reports/process-optimization) and [business orchestration](https://www.uipath.com/blog/product-and-updates/what-is-boat-business-orchestration-automation-technologies) are being sold less as point features and more as the control layer for AI in production.

## 2) Coherent storyline
This week’s signals point to a simple pattern: vendors are moving from “AI can do more” to “AI can do more safely, with context, governance, and workflow control.” That shows up in [Celonis’ 2026 Process Optimization Report](https://www.celonis.com/insights/reports/process-optimization), [SAP’s Q1 2026 newsroom activity](https://news.sap.com), and UiPath’s recent push across [agentic automation](https://www.uipath.com/blog), [retail pricing](https://www.uipath.com/blog/industry-solutions/retail-blind-spot-pricing-decisions-into-margin-leakage), and [agentic AI summit messaging](https://www.uipath.com/blog/ai/agentic-ai-summit-2026).

## 3) Most impacted stakeholder
[COO](https://www.uipath.com/blog/ai/agentic-ai-summit-2026) — the buying conversation is drifting toward operational reliability, exception handling, and measurable process outcomes rather than abstract AI experimentation.

## 4) Competitive watch
- [Celonis](https://www.celonis.com/insights/reports/process-optimization) is framing the category around agentic readiness and process ROI, which reinforces its position as the “brain layer” narrative in the market.
- [SAP Signavio](https://news.sap.com) remains close to the SAP core message: governance, business impact, and transformation execution, with newsroom activity suggesting continued emphasis on proof over hype.
- [UiPath](https://www.uipath.com/blog/product-and-updates/what-is-boat-business-orchestration-automation-technologies) is leaning hard into orchestration language, while posts on [performance testing](https://www.uipath.com/blog/ai/performance-testing-shouldnt-require-phd) and [retail margin leakage](https://www.uipath.com/blog/industry-solutions/retail-blind-spot-pricing-decisions-into-margin-leakage) show the company broadening beyond classic RPA.
- [Apromore](https://apromore.com/blog) is still worth watching as the “lighter-weight, operationally practical” alternative in process mining and AI-assisted process improvement.

## 5) Agentic data-model tooling watch
- [Collibra](https://www.collibra.com/blog/models-are-commodities-context-is-proprietary-why-context-engineering-is-the-new-business) is pushing “context engineering” as the differentiator, which keeps metadata and governance close to the agent stack.
- [dbt](https://www.getdbt.com/blog/tableau-and-dbt-mcps-together) is making MCP-style integrations part of its story, a sign that semantic orchestration is moving into mainstream analytics tooling.
- [Microsoft Fabric](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-fabric/blog/2025/12/17/fabcon-and-sqlcon-from-workshops-and-keynotes-to-demos-and-deep-dives/) keeps bundling data platform and AI narrative in a way that can absorb adjacent semantic tooling budgets.

## 6) Key implications
- Buyers are increasingly asking for AI that is governed by process and context, not just model capability.
- Competitive differentiation is shifting toward orchestration, observability, and safe production rollout.
- Konekti’s strongest angle remains “operational AI with control,” especially where compliance and handoffs matter.
