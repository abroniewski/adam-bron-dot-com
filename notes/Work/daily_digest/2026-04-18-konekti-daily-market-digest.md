# Konekti Daily Market Digest — 2026-04-18

## 1) What stood out
SAP and Celonis are both leaning harder into agentic execution language, while Apromore keeps framing process mining as operational infrastructure: [SAP Business AI Q1 2026 highlights](https://news.sap.com/2026/04/sap-business-ai-release-highlights-q1-2026/), [Celonis object-centric model versioning](https://docs.celonis.com/en/version-deploy-ocdm.html), and [Apromore’s ICPM 2026 takeaways](https://apromore.com/blog/from-hype-to-operational-reality-key-takeaways-from-icpm-2026).

## 2) Coherent storyline
The market is converging on the same message: process intelligence is no longer just about visibility. It is being packaged with execution, lifecycle control, and AI surfaces — from [Celonis Skills + Action Flows](https://docs.celonis.com/en/execute-action-flows-in-skills.html) to [SAP’s Joule/Business AI release highlights](https://news.sap.com/2026/04/sap-business-ai-release-highlights-q1-2026/) and [Google’s agent skills](https://developers.googleblog.com/closing-the-knowledge-gap-with-agent-skills/).

## 3) Most impacted stakeholder
**COO / process-excellence lead** — they will feel the pressure first because the buying conversation is shifting toward measurable intervention loops, not just dashboards or documentation.

## 4) Competitive watch
- **Celonis:** recent documentation updates around [object-centric data model versioning](https://docs.celonis.com/en/version-deploy-ocdm.html) and [package deployment](https://docs.celonis.com/en/version-deploy.html) suggest the platform is tightening governance around how models ship and evolve.
- **Celonis:** [machine learning application management](https://docs.celonis.com/en/creating-and-managing-machine-learning-applications.html) points to a more structured AI lifecycle story inside the process stack.
- **Celonis:** [Action Flows in Skills](https://docs.celonis.com/en/execute-action-flows-in-skills.html) is a useful signal that execution is being productized in a more user-facing way.
- **SAP Signavio / SAP:** [Business AI Q1 highlights](https://news.sap.com/2026/04/sap-business-ai-release-highlights-q1-2026/), [innovation award finalists](https://news.sap.com/2026/04/top-innovators-2026-sap-innovation-award-finalists/), and [award winners](https://news.sap.com/2026/04/honoring-2026-sap-innovation-award-winners/) keep the story tied to transformation outcomes and agentic workflows.
- **Apromore:** [ICPM 2026 takeaways](https://apromore.com/blog/from-hype-to-operational-reality-key-takeaways-from-icpm-2026) keep process mining framed as something operational teams can actually run, not just analyze.

## 5) Agentic data-model tooling watch
- Illumex remains strategically relevant via the [NVIDIA acquisition signal relayed through Cardumen Capital](https://www.prnewswire.com/apac/news-releases/cardumen-capital-strengthens-global-reach-through-its-taiwan-based-apac-partner-following-nvidias-acquisition-of-its-portfolio-company-illumex-302708212.html), which reinforces demand for governed semantic layers.

- Google’s [agent skills post](https://developers.googleblog.com/closing-the-knowledge-gap-with-agent-skills/) shows the builder ecosystem is normalizing reusable knowledge and evaluation primitives.

## 6) Key implications
- Buyers will increasingly expect process tools to explain and trigger action, not just report status.
- Semantic/data-model governance is becoming a competitive differentiator, not a back-office detail.
- Compliance pressure remains a practical trigger for procurement, especially in Europe: [EU Digital Omnibus / AI Act timelines](https://www.onetrust.com/blog/how-the-eu-digital-omnibus-reshapes-ai-act-timelines-and-governance-in-2026/) and [energy-sector AI deadlines](https://www.bakerbotts.com/thought-leadership/publications/2026/march/the-eu-ai-act).
