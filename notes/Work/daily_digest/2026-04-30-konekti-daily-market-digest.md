# Konekti Daily Market Digest — 2026-04-30

## 1) What stood out
Celonis keeps reinforcing the execution-gap narrative through official and third-party coverage, including its own [process intelligence framing](https://celonis.cio.com/cios-guide-to-process-intelligence-in-the-age-of-ai/free-the-process-breaking-system-silos-for-an-ai-ready-business/) and fresh market attention in [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/23/european-ai-unicorn-celonis-letter-from-london/).

## 2) Coherent storyline
The market is converging on one message: AI only matters when it is operationalized inside governed workflows. That shows up in Celonis’s “process layer” language, UiPath’s [BOAT](https://www.uipath.com/blog/product-and-updates/what-is-boat-business-orchestration-automation-technologies) framing, and its push toward [AgentOps](https://www.uipath.com/blog/ai/agent-ops-operationalizing-ai-agents-for-enterprise).

## 3) Most impacted stakeholder
The most impacted stakeholder is the **COO**, because the strongest signals are about execution quality, handoffs, and control across long-running business processes.

## 4) Competitive watch (Celonis / Signavio / UiPath / Apromore)
- **Celonis:** official and external coverage continues to position Celonis as the execution layer for enterprise AI, from [CIO commentary on process silos](https://celonis.cio.com/cios-guide-to-process-intelligence-in-the-age-of-ai/free-the-process-breaking-system-silos-for-an-ai-ready-business/) to broader market validation in [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/opinions/what-companies-are-getting-right-about-ai-in-2026/) and [BBC](https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/w3ct8g92).
- **SAP Signavio:** the procurement/compliance lane still matters because SAP’s ecosystem keeps moving toward AI-assisted process control; the adjacent signal here is the broader enterprise procurement AI conversation in [Computer Weekly](https://www.computerweekly.com/news/366641682/UK-governments-50m-sovereign-AI-fund-bids-to-commercialise-research).
- **UiPath:** UiPath is clearly sharpening the story around platform consolidation with [BOAT](https://www.uipath.com/blog/product-and-updates/what-is-boat-business-orchestration-automation-technologies), plus production readiness through [AgentOps](https://www.uipath.com/blog/ai/agent-ops-operationalizing-ai-agents-for-enterprise) and [AIUC-1](https://www.uipath.com/blog/product-and-updates/aiuc-1-certification-next-chapter-trusted-agentic-automation).
- **Apromore:** public signal is lighter today, so it remains the quietest of the set; the main takeaway is that process-mining buyers are moving toward more governed orchestration, not just dashboards.

## 5) Agentic data-model tooling watch (Illumex + peers)
The adjacent tooling signal is that enterprise teams want AI on top of a governed data foundation, not raw operational sprawl. A useful proxy is the procurement tooling discussion in [Inventive AI](https://www.inventive.ai/blog-posts/ai-tools-procurement-teams), where harmonized data and policy-aware agents are the selling points.

## 6) Key implications
- Lead with governance-first language; the winning pitch is now speed **plus** control.
- Position Konekti as the orchestration layer that makes AI useful in real workflows, not another point solution.
- Keep procurement/compliance and CIO-facing narratives tightly linked to execution outcomes.
