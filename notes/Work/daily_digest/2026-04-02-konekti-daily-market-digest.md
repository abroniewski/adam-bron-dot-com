# Konekti Daily Market Digest — 2026-04-02

## 1) What stood out (short)
UiPath quietly turned its WorkFusion acquisition into a production-ready financial-crime stack, with agentic reviewers shrinking sanctions cases from 20 minutes to ~2 minutes while humans only tackle exceptions ([TheElec](https://www.thelec.net/news/articleView.html?idxno=6253)). On the same news cycle SAP landed its 12th straight Leadership slot in Gartner’s TMS Quadrant with Joule-driven planning and AI-assisted goods receipt now positioned as table stakes ([SAP News](https://news.sap.com/2026/04/sap-a-leader-2026-gartner-magic-quadrant-tms/)).

## 2) Coherent storyline (brief)
Platform differentiation is collapsing into “governed autonomy”: SAP’s field leaders are telling customers their AI programs will fail without harmonized data and clean-core guardrails ([iTWire](https://itwire.com/business-it-news/data/sap-s-thomas-pfiester-your-ai-won-t-work-if-your-data-is-a-mess.html)), MariaDB is fusing OLTP + analytics + vector layers so agents don’t choke on ETL hops ([Efficiently Connected](https://www.efficientlyconnected.com/mariadb-bets-on-unified-data-layer-for-agentic-ai-scale/)), and developer tooling such as Coder is pulling MCP servers, policy-as-code, and approved assistants into one governed workbench ([GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/01/3266458/0/en/Coder-Secures-90M-Series-C-Led-by-KKR-to-Advance-Secure-Enterprise-AI-Development.html)).

## 3) Most impacted stakeholder (single)
**Enterprise procurement & compliance leads for process-intelligence suites** now have the hottest seat: federal CIOs say 80% of AI time is still being blown on data hygiene and inventory work ([Federal News Network](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/commentary/2026/04/the-data-challenge-impacting-federal-ai-adoption/)), while Vision Compliance’s new EU AI Act benchmark shows 78% of enterprises lack even a basic AI system register or owner ([NatLawReview](https://natlawreview.com/press-releases/vision-compliance-releases-2026-eu-ai-act-readiness-report-finds-78)).

## 4) Competitive watch (Celonis / Signavio / UiPath / Apromore)
- **Celonis:** ValueSelling’s APAC expansion just poached longtime Celonis manufacturing lead Tetsuro Yamamoto to run enterprise enablement, signaling that customers still need heavy human coaching to convert Celonis licenses into savings ([SalesTechStar](https://salestechstar.com/sales-engagement/valueselling-associates-expands-its-global-sales-training-presence-with-strategic-growth-in-the-apac-region/)).
- **Signavio (SAP):** Thomas Pfiester is reframing Signavio + LeanIX as the data-ready spine for SAP’s new Foundation/Advanced/Max success tiers so customers can switch on Joule agents without hallucinations ([iTWire](https://itwire.com/business-it-news/data/sap-s-thomas-pfiester-your-ai-won-t-work-if-your-data-is-a-mess.html)).
- **UiPath:** The WorkFusion-powered banking agents now automate sanctions screening, adverse media checks, and loan-file QA, pushing UiPath deeper into financial crime operations where Celonis and Signavio still rely on partners ([TheElec](https://www.thelec.net/news/articleView.html?idxno=6253)).
- **Apromore:** Salesforce’s March 27 inducement RSUs for Apromore hires show Benioff is still paying to keep that process-mining brain trust in-house while Agentforce wins VA and DOL workloads ([Markets Daily](https://www.themarketsdaily.com/2026/03/27/salesforce-nysecrm-shares-down-3-5-should-you-sell.html)).

## 5) Agentic data-model tooling watch (Illumex + peers)
- **Unified data layers:** MariaDB’s GridGain deal chases Illumex-style semantic agility by collapsing OLTP, cache, and analytics tiers into one agent-ready fabric ([Efficiently Connected](https://www.efficientlyconnected.com/mariadb-bets-on-unified-data-layer-for-agentic-ai-scale/)).
- **Governed dev environments:** Coder’s $90M round lets enterprises auto-provision MCP servers, approved copilots, and secure sandboxes so agent builders stay inside policy guardrails ([GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/01/3266458/0/en/Coder-Secures-90M-Series-C-Led-by-KKR-to-Advance-Secure-Enterprise-AI-Development.html)).
- **Industry playbooks:** Next week’s Future of Data & AI conference is dedicating half its agenda to zero-trust agent architectures, Docker sandboxes, and MCP toolchains, reinforcing how mainstream the guardrail conversation has become ([Data Science Dojo](https://datasciencedojo.com/agentic-ai-conference/)).
- **Prompt-layer risk:** Building Creative Machines’ April brief is now treating MCP permissions and system prompts as “crown jewels” after the Lilli breach narrative, aligning with Illumex’s thesis that semantic context must be governed like infra ([Building Creative Machines](https://www.buildingcreativemachines.com/p/ai-and-creativity-monthly-brief-april)).

## 6) Key implications (max 3 bullets)
- Buyers will reward vendors that show a packaged “data readiness + activation” motion—SAP is already bundling unlimited AI activation sessions, so Konekti should pair every pitch with tangible clean-core gap assessments ([iTWire](https://itwire.com/business-it-news/data/sap-s-thomas-pfiester-your-ai-won-t-work-if-your-data-is-a-mess.html)).
- Expect RFPs to ask how agent pipelines are governed end-to-end; pointing to unified data layers (MariaDB), governed dev envs (Coder), and zero-trust playbooks (Data Science Dojo agenda) can differentiate our GTM positioning.
- Compliance teams will increasingly own the veto: the EU AI Act readiness gap plus new AI due-diligence checklists mean we need ready-made evidence packages (inventories, traceability, failure-mode tests) baked into deliverables ([NatLawReview](https://natlawreview.com/press-releases/vision-compliance-releases-2026-eu-ai-act-readiness-report-finds-78); [Security Boulevard](https://securityboulevard.com/2026/04/ai-due-diligence-checklist-2026-how-to-avoid-ai-implementation-failures-security-risks-and-cost-overruns/)).
