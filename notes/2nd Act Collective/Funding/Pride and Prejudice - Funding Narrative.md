# Pride and Prejudice (2026)

_A Participatory Cultural Programme for Belonging in Brabant_

---

## Heritage

Pride and Prejudice is used as a living cultural lens to explore how identity, class, and societal expectations shape human interaction, both historically and today.

While widely recognised in international communities, this work is less embedded in Dutch cultural contexts. By presenting it through a locally developed adaptation, the project introduces an important piece of international literary heritage to new audiences, making it accessible and relevant within Brabant.

Through workshops, discussion groups, and the rehearsal process itself, participants engage with themes such as social status, gender roles, and first impressions. These themes are not treated as historical artefacts, but as entry points to reflect on contemporary society. Participants are encouraged to draw parallels between the rigid social structures of the 19th century and the more subtle, but still present, dynamics of modern life.

In doing so, the project activates heritage as a tool for understanding current social realities, rather than simply preserving the past.

---

## Culture

This project expands cultural participation beyond the traditional model of performers and spectators by creating multiple, accessible entry points into the experience.

Participants can engage through:
- Performance and production roles
- Reading groups and literary discussions in collaboration with local partners such as independent bookstores
- Workshops exploring social interaction and behaviour through a historical lens
- Contributions to costume, props, and production elements through wider community networks

This layered approach allows individuals to participate at different levels, depending on their interests and confidence. It creates opportunities for people who would not typically engage with theatre to take part in a meaningful way.

A key outcome observed in earlier iterations of this project is the connection between international participants and existing Dutch communities with a strong interest in literature and historical culture. By anchoring the programme in a shared point of interest, the project creates a natural bridge between groups that might not otherwise interact.

In this way, culture becomes not just an output, but a mechanism for connection.

---

## Lifestyle

Rather than existing as a single event, the project unfolds as a continuous social and cultural experience.

Participants move through different forms of engagement over time:
- Exploring themes through reading and discussion
- Engaging in workshops and collaborative activities
- Contributing creatively to the production
- Sharing a collective experience during the performance

This structure supports repeated interaction and helps participants build relationships over time. For many, the project acts as an entry point into a wider network of communities, whether within ITCE or through partner organisations.

The result is not only artistic participation, but the development of social connections, confidence, and a sense of belonging. This is particularly relevant for individuals who are new to the region or seeking ways to integrate into local communities.

---

## Strategic Contribution to a Liveable Brabant

This project uses culture as a practical tool to strengthen social cohesion and broaden participation in Brabant.

Its strategic contribution lies in:
- Lowering barriers to participation by offering multiple entry points beyond performance
- Connecting different communities, particularly Dutch and international residents, through shared cultural interests
- Encouraging collaboration across sectors, including cultural organisations, literary spaces, and community networks
- Supporting wellbeing and inclusion through structured opportunities for creative and social engagement

Importantly, the project does not position theatre as an end in itself, but as a framework for interaction. By using a well-known narrative as a shared reference point, it enables participants to engage with broader societal questions in an accessible and structured way.

The approach is intentionally repeatable and forms part of a broader effort to build sustainable, community-driven cultural programming in the region.

---

## One-line positioning

Pride and Prejudice is reimagined as a participatory cultural programme that uses a classical work of heritage to explore belonging, connect communities, and expand access to cultural participation in Brabant.
