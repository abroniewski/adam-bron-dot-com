Here’s a **standard business plan outline**, drawing from best practices in startup planning (Y Combinator, *The Lean Startup*) and adapted for creative or community ventures like yours :

---

### 1. Executive Summary

* **Purpose & vision** — Why this venture exists and the impact you aim to create.
* **Summary of the offer** — What you do (productions, workshops, programs).
* **Current stage & goals** — Where you are now and what you need next (funding, partners, validation).

---

### 2. Company Overview

* **Mission statement** — A clear, memorable purpose statement.
* **Founding story & values** — Origin and guiding principles (e.g., inclusion, artistic growth, community).
* **Legal structure & ownership** — Foundation, collective, or for-profit model.
* **Location & reach** — Where you operate and your target community or region.

---

### 3. Market & Audience Analysis

* **Target participants/audiences** — Who you serve (actors, learners, local audiences).
* **Market need** — The social or cultural gaps you address (e.g., limited semi-pro theatre access, community connection).
* **Comparable organizations** — Local or international peers, what they do well or miss.
* **Trends & insights** — Audience preferences, participation patterns, demand for creative wellbeing experiences.

---

### 4. Programs & Offerings

* **Productions** — Artistic focus, frequency, audience segments.
* **Workshops & training** — Skills development, community engagement, revenue model.
* **Community engagement** — Volunteer pathways, feedback channels, inclusion model.
* **Future programs** — Pilots or experiments planned (e.g., new “boot camp” weekends).

---

### 5. Value Proposition

* **For participants** — Belonging, creative expression, skill-building.
* **For audiences** — High-quality English-language theatre and local cultural identity.
* **For partners/funders** — Community impact, local talent development, social cohesion.
* **Unique advantage** — How your model differs from other theatre groups (portfolio approach, safe creative culture, inclusivity) .

---

### 6. Strategy & Business Model

* **Revenue streams** — Ticket sales, workshops, memberships, grants, sponsorships, cross-subsidy from popular shows.
* **Cost structure** — Key expenses (venues, rights, marketing, production).
* **Growth strategy** — How you’ll test, learn, and expand (aligning with *Lean Startup*’s “Build–Measure–Learn” cycle).
* **Key milestones** — 6-month and 12-month goals (e.g., workshop profitability, community engagement metrics).

---

### 7. Organization & Governance

* **Team roles & responsibilities** — Artistic, community, and operational leads.
* **Governance model** — Board composition, decision-making processes, inclusion mechanisms.
* **Partnerships** — Collaborations with theatres, cultural foundations, municipalities, or sponsors.

---

### 8. Marketing & Communications

* **Brand positioning** — Voice, tone, and identity (e.g., “Brainport’s English-speaking theatre community”).
* **Audience development plan** — Surveys, social media, mailing list strategy.
* **Engagement funnel** — From audience → participant → repeat contributor.

---

### 9. Financial Plan

* **Startup costs** — One-time and recurring.
* **Budget forecasts** — Income and expenses for 1–3 years.
* **Break-even analysis** — When activities become self-sustaining.
* **Funding strategy** — Grants, memberships, in-kind support, sponsorship tiers.

---

### 10. Risk Management & Evaluation

* **Key risks** — Financial, operational, governance, cultural.
* **Mitigation plans** — Contingencies, reserves, documentation.
* **Success metrics** — Artistic quality, community retention, financial sustainability, inclusivity indicators.
